# CS459 - Investment Behavior & Preferences Analysis

## Project Overview
This is a Business Intelligence project analyzing investment behavior of 40 investors using a survey dataset (CS459 – Spring 2026).

The goal is to understand how demographic factors, risk profiles, and financial goals influence investment choices across different financial products.

---

## Objective
To identify patterns in investor behavior and provide data-driven insights that help match the right investment product with the right customer segment.

---

## Key Areas of Analysis
- Investor demographics (age, gender)
- Investment preferences (Mutual Funds, Equity, Fixed Deposits, Gold, Bonds)
- Risk profiling and return expectations
- Behavioral patterns and financial goals
- Marketing channel effectiveness

---

## Data Processing
- Checked and cleaned dataset (no missing values or duplicates)
- Standardized categorical values
- Fixed column naming issues
- Validated ranking and age constraints
- Feature engineering (risk score, age groups, engagement score, etc.)

---

## Analysis & Methods
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Feature Engineering
- Statistical Tests:
  - Chi-Square Test
  - T-Test
  - ANOVA
  - Pearson Correlation
- Power BI Dashboard Development

---

## Dashboards Built
- Executive Overview Dashboard  
- Investment Preferences Dashboard  
- Risk & Behavior Analysis Dashboard  
- Marketing Channel Effectiveness Dashboard  

---

## Key Insights
- Investment preferences vary strongly by age and risk profile  
- Younger investors prefer higher-risk products like Equity  
- Fixed Deposits are preferred by low-risk investors  
- Risk-return expectations are often misaligned  
- Financial consultants are the most trusted information source  

---

## Conclusion
The project highlights that investment behavior is highly personalized and influenced by multiple factors. A data-driven approach can significantly improve product matching and financial decision-making.
